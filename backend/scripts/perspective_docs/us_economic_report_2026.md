# Economic Report of the President 2026

## Economic Framework: America First Economic Policy
The 2026 Economic Report presents the Trump Administration's first full year of economic policy. The central narrative frames the Biden economy as having delivered 40-year inflation highs, real wage decline, and government-driven job growth — and Trump's policies as correcting this through tax cuts, deregulation, trade tariffs, and border control. The CEA estimates the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) will increase real GDP by 4.6–4.9% over four years (approximately 1.1–1.2% higher average annual growth).

## One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA)
The OBBBA is framed as the largest tax cut in American history, permanently extending TCJA provisions and adding new relief: no tax on tips, no tax on overtime, tax relief for seniors, a doubled Child Tax Credit, and a $1,000 Trump Account for every American child born 2025–2028. The CEA projects the OBBBA will boost average real wages by $4,000–$7,200 per worker after four years and reduce the federal deficit to 94% of GDP (versus 117% under TCJA-expiration baseline).

## Trade Policy and Tariffs
Tariffs are presented as having catalysed trade deals that open foreign markets to American firms, reduce the trade deficit, and attract trillions in pledged US investment. The report frames past "free trade" as enabling unfair foreign practices — China's non-market economy policies, tariff asymmetries, and supply chain capture are cited. Tariff revenue is presented as a fiscal tool and negotiating leverage simultaneously.

## Key Themes by Chapter
Chapters cover: OBBBA economic effects, regulatory reform (10:1 deregulation ratio), trade policy, energy dominance, AI revolution, housing, industrial supply chains, defence industrial base, labour market, critique of DEI policies, healthcare competition, private equity access, ESG investment critique, and the 2025 year in review. The defence industrial base chapter directly links economic reindustrialisation to national security.

## Implications for Intelligence Collection
The 2026 Economic Report signals US economic policy priorities that affect allied and adversary calculations: tariff leverage creates trade negotiation intelligence value; defence industrial base investment signals procurement priorities; AI and technology investment reveals US competitive strategy in the technology race; and the energy dominance agenda has direct implications for global LNG markets and allied energy security.
