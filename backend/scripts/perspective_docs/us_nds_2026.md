# 2026 National Defense Strategy

## Strategic Direction: Peace Through Strength
The 2026 NDS is issued by the Department of War (formerly Department of Defense, renamed under Trump) under Secretary Pete Hegseth. It frames the strategy as a rejection of "grandiose strategies of the past" in favour of "flexible, practical realism" that prioritises the gravest threats to Americans' interests. Four Lines of Effort structure the strategy: defend the US Homeland; deter China in the Indo-Pacific through strength, not confrontation; increase burden-sharing with US allies and partners; and supercharge the US Defence Industrial Base.

## Line of Effort 1: Defend the Homeland
Homeland defence is the foremost military priority. This includes: securing borders against narco-terrorists and illegal migration (with DoW supporting DHS); Golden Dome for America missile defence system; countering unmanned aerial threats; modernising nuclear forces; bolstering cyber defences; and countering Islamic terrorists capable of striking the Homeland. The Trump Corollary to the Monroe Doctrine authorises decisive unilateral action throughout the Western Hemisphere — demonstrated by operations SOUTHERN SPEAR, ABSOLUTE RESOLVE, and MIDNIGHT HAMMER (Iran's nuclear infrastructure).

## Line of Effort 2: Deter China
China is named the "most powerful state relative to us since the 19th century" and the primary strategic challenge. US policy seeks a "decent peace" — not Chinese humiliation or regime change — but will erect a strong denial defence along the First Island Chain (FIC) and enable regional allies to do more. The goal is deterrence by denial: making aggression not worth attempting. Military-to-military communications with the PLA are to be expanded for deconfliction.

## Line of Effort 3: Burden-Sharing
Allies must take primary responsibility for their own regions: European NATO allies for conventional European defence (with critical but more limited US support); South Korea for the Korean Peninsula; Israel and Gulf partners for the Middle East; African partners for counterterrorism. NATO's Hague Summit standard of 5% GDP total security spending (3.5% hard military + 1.5% security-related) is the new benchmark.

## Line of Effort 4: Defence Industrial Base
The NDS calls for a "once-in-a-century revival" of the US defence industry — rebuilding production capacity, empowering innovators, adopting AI, and enabling allied production. The DIB is framed as the foundation of US military power and the linchpin of the alliance system.

## Implications for Intelligence Collection
The 2026 NDS reshapes the intelligence collection framework: Indo-Pacific primacy shifts resources from Europe; allied burden-sharing compliance becomes an assessment requirement; the Homeland-focused posture elevates domestic security intelligence; and adversary exploitation of the European focus reduction is a key warning indicator requirement.
