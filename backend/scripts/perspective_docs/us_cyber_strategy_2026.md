# President Trump's Cyber Strategy for America

## Strategic Vision
Published in March 2026, this strategy frames cyberspace as central to US economic, military, and technological dominance — and as a domain under persistent adversary attack. It positions the Trump Administration as taking a more aggressive and proactive posture than previous administrations, citing specific operations: destroying online scammers' networks and seizing $15 billion, Operation MIDNIGHT HAMMER (Iran's nuclear infrastructure), and cyber operations supporting narco-terrorist interdiction. The strategy explicitly supports NSS goals of putting America first in cyberspace.

## Six Policy Pillars

**1. Shape Adversary Behavior**: Deploy full US government defensive and offensive cyber operations; dismantle adversary networks; impose costs through all instruments of national power; uproot criminal infrastructure; counter surveillance-state and authoritarian technologies.

**2. Promote Common Sense Regulation**: Streamline cyber regulations to reduce compliance burdens; emphasise US privacy rights; reduce regulatory barriers that slow industry response to threats.

**3. Modernise Federal Government Networks**: Implement zero-trust architecture, post-quantum cryptography, and cloud transition; use AI-powered cybersecurity to defend federal networks; modernise procurement to access best technology.

**4. Secure Critical Infrastructure**: Harden energy grids, financial systems, telecoms, data centres, water utilities, and hospitals; remove adversary vendors (Huawei, ZTE, etc.) from critical systems; ensure OT/ICS supply chain security.

**5. Sustain Superiority in Critical and Emerging Technologies**: Secure AI technology stack; implement AI-enabled cyber defence tools; promote post-quantum cryptography adoption; secure cryptocurrency and blockchain; call out foreign AI platforms that censor, surveil, and mislead.

**6. Build Talent and Capacity**: Develop US cyber workforce pipeline across academia, vocational training, and private sector; eliminate barriers between industry, academia, government, and military.

## Implications for Intelligence Collection
The strategy signals US offensive cyber posture and priority targets — adversary networks conducting economic espionage, critical infrastructure pre-positioning, and disinformation operations. Collection priorities include adversary responses to US offensive actions, the resilience of Chinese and Russian cyber operations to US disruption, and allied cyber capability development.
