# Annual Progress Report on the Strategic Compass for Security and Defence (2025)

## Three Years On: Accelerating Urgency
Three years after the adoption of the Strategic Compass, the EU faces what the High Representative describes as "an unprecedented combination of security threats and challenges" — from high-intensity warfare to hybrid activities targeting critical infrastructure. The 2025 report calls for faster progress on defence readiness, noting that while many Compass goals have been implemented, the pace must increase given the deteriorating security environment. EU military support to Ukraine now exceeds €49 billion since 2022 and over 71,000 Ukrainian soldiers have been trained under the EU Military Assistance Mission.

## Act: Rapid Deployment Capacity
The EU Rapid Deployment Capacity (EU RDC) has reached initial operating capability at 5,000 troops. The focus has shifted to increasing exercise frequency, improving command-and-control arrangements, and ensuring the force can deploy in the timeframes required for crisis response. The report notes the EU must develop more efficient and robust engagement instruments for the full spectrum of crisis scenarios.

## Secure: Hybrid Threats and Resilience
Hybrid threats — including foreign information manipulation, interference in democratic processes, and attacks on critical infrastructure (cables, pipelines, space assets) — have intensified. The report emphasises EU-NATO coordination on hybrid threat response, building intelligence-sharing capacity through EU INTCEN, and strengthening maritime and space domain awareness.

## Invest: Defence Industrial Base
Member states have significantly increased defence budgets since 2022. The EU Defence Industrial Strategy and EDIP (European Defence Industrial Programme) are designed to incentivise joint procurement, pool demand, and build sustained European production capacity. The Act in Support of Ammunition Production (ASAP) was activated to address the Ukraine ammunition shortage, delivering over 1 million rounds in 2024.

## Partner: Strategic Partnerships
EU-NATO complementarity is reinforced. New bilateral partnerships with Japan, South Korea, and the Gulf states reflect the EU's growing engagement in the Indo-Pacific. Ukraine's defence integration with the European Defence Technological and Industrial Base is a priority.

## Implications for Intelligence Collection
The Compass progress report reveals EU capability investments and shortfalls — particularly in strategic enablers, hybrid threat detection, and space domain awareness. Adversary collection targets include EU RDC readiness timelines, intelligence-sharing arrangements within EU INTCEN, and the pace of EU defence industrial output.
