# RESourceEU Action Plan

## Strategic Context
The RESourceEU Action Plan applies the logic of REPowerEU — which successfully phased out Russian gas dependency — to critical raw materials (CRMs). The EU is excessively dependent on China for most CRMs, including 26 relevant for the energy transition, across all value chain stages from extraction to magnet manufacturing. China has weaponised this dominance through a series of export controls on graphite, gallium, germanium, tungsten, rare earths, and related equipment. The October 2025 restrictions included extraterritorial controls on products containing certain CRMs.

## Critical Raw Materials Act Targets
The CRMA (2024) sets binding supply security targets for 2030: 10% of EU annual consumption extracted domestically, 40% processed domestically, 25% recycled, and no more than 65% sourced from any single third country. RESourceEU accelerates implementation of these targets through mobilisation of public and private actors and a broad policy toolkit.

## De-risking Measures
The Action Plan combines supply-side measures (new extraction partnerships with resource-rich nations, EU critical raw materials club), demand-side measures (circular economy and recycling mandates, material efficiency standards), and financial instruments (de-risking guarantees, European Investment Bank support for CRM projects). It also targets administrative bottlenecks that delay permitting for European extraction projects.

## Geopolitical Framing
The document frames CRM dependency as directly threatening EU manufacturing competitiveness, defence and aerospace supply chains, and clean energy transition. China's Non-Market Economy Policies — subsidies, predatory pricing, forced technology transfers, and export controls — are identified as creating structural dependencies that constitute a security threat.

## Implications for Intelligence Collection
EU collection priorities include monitoring China's export control regime and enforcement patterns, identifying alternative supplier nations and their vulnerabilities, tracking Chinese investment in CRM processing capacity in third countries, and assessing the resilience of EU defence-critical supply chains (rare earth magnets, gallium-based semiconductors, graphite for batteries).
