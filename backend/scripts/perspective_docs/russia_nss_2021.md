# Strategy of National Security of the Russian Federation (2021)

## Strategic Framework
Approved by Presidential Decree No. 400 of July 2, 2021, this Strategy is Russia's foundational national security planning document, superseding the 2015 version. It defines national interests, strategic priorities, and policy objectives across nine domains: preservation of the Russian people, national defence, state and public security, information security, economic security, scientific and technological development, environmental safety, traditional values protection, and strategic stability.

## Anti-Western Framing
The 2021 Strategy frames Western countries as pursuing a "deliberate policy to contain the Russian Federation." It identifies hostile actions including sanctions, regime change promotion, military exercises near Russian borders, information campaigns creating a "hostile image of Russia," and restrictions on Russian language and media. The desire to "isolate the Russian Federation" is presented as hampering multilateral cooperation on terrorism, cybercrime, and health crises.

## National Defence Priorities
The Strategy identifies NATO's eastward expansion and military infrastructure build-up near Russian borders as a primary military threat, alongside US abandonment of arms control commitments and deployment of intermediate-range missiles in Europe. National defence goals include strategic deterrence (nuclear and conventional), maintaining nuclear parity, and preparing the Russian economy for wartime mobilisation. Particular emphasis is placed on technological sovereignty in defence production.

## Information Security
Russia frames control of its information space as a core national security function. Protecting society from "destructive information and psychological impact" and ensuring sovereignty over Russian segments of the internet are explicit priorities. External information operations targeting Russian audiences are categorised as a national security threat equivalent to military threats.

## Economic and Technological Security
The Strategy emphasises reducing dependence on imports in key economic sectors, increasing food and energy self-sufficiency, and developing Russia's scientific and technological base. These priorities — written in 2021 — directly prefigure the policies Russia implemented after 2022 sanctions were imposed.

## Implications for Intelligence Collection
The 2021 NSS provides the ideological and policy foundation for Russian behaviour since the 2022 invasion: nuclear signalling, information operations, sanctions circumvention, and technological self-reliance are all directly authorised by this document. Collection priorities include Russian compliance with (or departure from) its stated strategic deterrence posture, implementation of economic security measures, and the gap between stated traditional values framing and operational intelligence activity.
