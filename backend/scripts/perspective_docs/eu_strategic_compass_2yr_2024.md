# Annual Progress Report on the Strategic Compass for Security and Defence (2024)

## Two Years After Adoption
The 2024 report covers implementation of the Strategic Compass adopted in March 2022. The High Representative (Josep Borrell) frames the report around the EU's response to Russia's war of aggression against Ukraine and the increasingly alarming global threat landscape. Total EU and member state support to Ukraine stands at €88 billion, with €28 billion in military assistance. More than 60,000 Ukrainian soldiers trained on EU soil by mid-2024.

## Act: CSDP Missions and Operations
The EU has launched EUNAVFOR ASPIDES in the Red Sea for maritime security against Houthi attacks. Existing CSDP missions — Atalanta (anti-piracy), Irini (Libya arms embargo), and Gulf of Guinea coordinated maritime presence — reflect the EU's growing role as a global maritime security provider. The report acknowledges difficult decisions facing missions in the Sahel following coups in Mali, Burkina Faso, and Niger.

## Secure: Intelligence Capacities and Hybrid Threats
EU INTCEN has expanded its situational awareness function. The EU is investing in counter-disinformation, election security, and critical infrastructure protection. A Strategic Compass priority is boosting satellite-based ISR capability and improving maritime and airspace domain awareness through EU member state contributions.

## Invest: Defence Expenditure and the EDF
EU member states increased defence spending by an average of 20% in 2023. The European Defence Fund (€8 billion, 2021–2027) is funding over 60 collaborative R&D programmes. EDIRPA and ASAP are activating joint procurement and ammunition production acceleration. The 2024 report highlights persistent capability gaps in air defence, precision strike, and strategic enablers.

## Partner: EU-NATO Complementarity
The 2024 report emphasises the EU-NATO complementarity framework and the importance of avoiding duplication. European Sky Shield Initiative (ESSI) — outside the EU framework but involving EU members — is noted as a significant air defence investment. Indo-Pacific partnerships with Japan, South Korea, and Australia are deepening.

## Implications for Intelligence Collection
The 2024 report identifies EU capability gaps that represent targeting opportunities for adversaries: the air defence shortfall (before ESSI begins to close it), limited European strategic lift, and persistent dependence on US satellite intelligence. Collection priorities include European defence industrial ramp-up capacity and the coherence of EU-NATO joint planning.
