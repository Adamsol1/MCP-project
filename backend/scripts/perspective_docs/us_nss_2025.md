# National Security Strategy of the United States of America (2025)

## Strategic Philosophy: America First Realism
The 2025 NSS frames post-Cold War US foreign policy as having "gone astray" — pursuing global domination, nation-building, and multilateral institutions that eroded American power. President Trump's correction is described as "flexible, practical realism" anchored in "America First" — evaluating all foreign policy through the lens of concrete American interests. The strategy explicitly rejects "utopian idealism" and the rules-based international order framing, instead emphasising sovereignty, balance of power, and non-interventionism as a default predisposition.

## Core National Interests
The NSS identifies five core foreign policy priorities: (1) ensuring the Western Hemisphere remains stable and free of hostile foreign control, enforcing a "Trump Corollary to the Monroe Doctrine"; (2) keeping the Indo-Pacific free and open, halting damage from foreign actors to the US economy; (3) supporting European allies while restoring Europe's "civilisational self-confidence"; (4) preventing adversarial power from dominating the Middle East without "forever wars"; and (5) ensuring US technology standards — particularly in AI, biotech, and quantum computing — drive global norms.

## Strategic Principles
Key principles include: focused definition of national interest (not everything is a core security interest), peace through strength, predisposition to non-interventionism, flexible realism, primacy of nation-states over international institutions, protection of US sovereignty from transnational organisations, maintaining balance of power to prevent adversarial dominance, and fairness in alliances and trade (allies must spend more on defence).

## Regional Approach
**Western Hemisphere**: Monroe Doctrine enforcement, narco-terrorist operations, canal and Arctic access. **Asia**: Maintaining Indo-Pacific balance of power — China as the primary strategic challenge. **Europe**: Allies take primary responsibility for conventional defence; US provides critical but reduced support; Russia is a "persistent but manageable threat" for European allies. **Middle East**: Regional allies take the lead; US avoids forever wars while maintaining Iran containment.

## Implications for Intelligence Collection
The 2025 NSS reshapes US intelligence priorities: China in the Indo-Pacific (Taiwan contingency, economic coercion) replaces Europe as the primary focus; burden-sharing compliance of NATO allies becomes an assessment priority; the Monroe Doctrine corollary requires enhanced collection on Latin American adversary influence; and the technology competition framing elevates AI, biotech, and quantum as collection domains.
