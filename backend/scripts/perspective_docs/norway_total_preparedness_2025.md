# Total Preparedness: Prepared for Crises and War

## Strategic Context
This white paper represents the Norwegian government's most comprehensive reform of civil preparedness since World War II. It is explicitly framed in the context of Russia's war against Ukraine, intensified hybrid threats, and the growing risk that Norway could face armed conflict. The document states that Norway's preparedness model must plan for "acts of war on Norwegian territory as well as on allied territory" — a departure from post-Cold War assumptions.

## Three Primary Objectives
Norwegian civil society must be: (1) equipped to deal with a crisis or war, (2) able to withstand hybrid threats, and (3) able to support military efforts. These objectives are operationalised through seven strategic directions covering local preparedness, collective resource mobilisation, cyber resilience, national control of critical infrastructure, supply security, civil-military cooperation, and allied support capacity within NATO.

## Hybrid Threat Resilience
The document dedicates substantial attention to hybrid threats: disinformation, influence operations, covert investments in strategic businesses, supply chain disruptions, insider risk, and cyberattacks. State actors are identified as using "profit-motivated criminals to carry out operations." Specific measures include countering insider threats in critical undertakings, strengthening situational awareness, and building population resilience to influence operations.

## Critical Infrastructure and National Control
Norway is strengthening national control over critical infrastructure, natural resources, property, and strategically important undertakings. This includes investment control (screening of foreign acquisitions), cyber security standards, and Svalbard as a strategically sensitive area requiring enhanced attention. The energy sector — Norwegian gas supplies are critical to European security — is specifically highlighted as vulnerable to pressure and sabotage.

## Civil-Military Integration and Total Defence
The white paper strengthens the "total defence" concept — coordinated use of civilian and military resources. Key priorities include military mobility improvements, the High North (Troms and Finnmark) as the primary strategic region, and pre-positioning for allied reinforcement. A National Preparedness and Response System is being established.

## Implications for Intelligence Collection
The white paper maps Norwegian vulnerabilities explicitly: subsea infrastructure (pipelines, cables), digitally dependent critical services, Svalbard's strategic sensitivity, and hybrid threat exposure through disinformation channels. Norwegian collection priorities — and adversary targeting interests — include energy infrastructure security, NATO reinforcement reception capacity in northern Norway, and insider threat risk in critical sectors.
