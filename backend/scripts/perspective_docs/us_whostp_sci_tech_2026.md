# Trump Administration Science & Technology Highlights: Year One

## Strategic Framework
Published by the White House OSTP in January 2026 covering the first year of Trump's second term, this document catalogues science and technology achievements across AI, quantum technology, nuclear technology, biotechnology, advanced transportation, space, spectrum/6G, advanced manufacturing, and critical minerals. The strategic framing positions US technology dominance as foundational to economic prosperity, national security, and the American way of life.

## Artificial Intelligence
AI is the document's dominant theme, covering: AI innovation and R&D; federal AI adoption; AI infrastructure (data centres, energy supply); semiconductors and AI chips; international AI engagement (promoting US AI standards globally and countering Chinese AI platforms with embedded censorship and surveillance); AI workforce development; and AI safety for children. The administration has rolled back Biden-era AI executive orders as "burdensome" and is pursuing a lighter-touch regulatory environment to accelerate private sector innovation.

## Quantum Technology
The US is advancing quantum R&D, quantum computing, advanced quantum applications, and quantum commercialisation. Post-quantum cryptography deployment across federal systems is a priority. The strategy frames quantum as both an economic opportunity and a security necessity — particularly given adversary quantum computing programmes.

## Nuclear Technologies
Nuclear energy, nuclear power applications, and nuclear fusion R&D are highlighted. The administration is pursuing nuclear energy as part of energy dominance, supporting advanced reactor deployment and fusion research. This links to the AI infrastructure chapter: AI data centres require massive power, and nuclear is framed as the solution.

## Space and Critical Minerals
Space exploration, commercial space, space security, and spectrum/6G are covered. Critical minerals — including rare earths — are addressed in the context of supply chain security and reducing Chinese dominance of processing capacity. This links directly to the EU's RESourceEU priorities.

## Implications for Intelligence Collection
The OSTP document reveals US technology investment priorities and competitive strategy: AI infrastructure concentration (data centres, chip supply) as strategic vulnerabilities; the international AI engagement pillar signals efforts to establish US AI standards as global norms, creating friction with China and EU regulatory approaches; quantum computing timelines affect cryptographic security assessments; and nuclear energy revival intersects with non-proliferation intelligence priorities.
