# Financial Sector Threat Landscape

## Strategic Overview
The financial sector is a target for both strategic disruption and criminal profit. State actors seek to demonstrate coercive capacity, evade sanctions, and undermine confidence in Western financial systems. Criminal actors — frequently operating with state tolerance — conduct ransomware and fraud for direct financial gain.

## Key Threat Vectors

### SWIFT and Payment System Targeting
SWIFT exclusion has been used as a sanctions instrument against Russia (2022) and Iran. In response, sanctioned states develop alternative payment mechanisms (SPFS, CIPS) and exploit cryptocurrency for sanctions evasion. The SWIFT network itself has been targeted — the 2016 Bangladesh Bank heist routed $81 million through the SWIFT system via Lazarus Group (North Korea).

### Central Bank and Reserve Targeting
The freezing of approximately $300 billion in Russian central bank reserves held in Western institutions (2022) demonstrated the vulnerability of sovereign assets to political action. This has accelerated diversification away from dollar-denominated reserves among non-aligned states.

### Ransomware Against Financial Institutions
Banks, insurers, and payment processors are high-value ransomware targets. Operational disruption — even brief — has severe reputational and regulatory consequences, increasing ransom payment rates. LockBit, BlackCat, and Russia-nexus groups have targeted major financial institutions across Europe and North America.

### Cryptocurrency and Sanctions Evasion
Russia, Iran, and North Korea use cryptocurrency — particularly privacy coins and chain-mixing services — to evade sanctions and move illicit funds. North Korea's Lazarus Group has stolen over $3 billion in cryptocurrency since 2017, partially funding weapons programmes.

## Known Incidents
- Bangladesh Bank SWIFT heist ($81M stolen, North Korea attributed, 2016)
- Lazarus Group targeting of cryptocurrency exchanges and DeFi protocols (ongoing, 2017–present)
- Ransomware attack on ION Trading Technologies disrupting derivatives market reporting (2023)

## Intelligence Implications
Financial system integrity is both a strategic asset and a collection target. Intelligence priorities include: sanctions evasion networks, state-sponsored theft funding weapons programmes, and pre-positioned access in financial infrastructure for contingency disruption.
