# Energy Sector Threat Landscape

## Strategic Overview
Energy infrastructure is among the highest-value targets for state and state-sponsored actors. Disruption achieves simultaneous economic, political, and psychological effects. The sector's increasing digitalisation — SCADA systems, smart grids, remote monitoring — has expanded the attack surface while dependencies on cross-border pipelines and interconnectors create cascading failure potential.

## Key Threat Vectors

### Critical Infrastructure Attacks
Offshore oil and gas platforms, onshore LNG terminals, pipeline compressor stations, and electricity grid substations are high-value physical targets. The Nord Stream sabotage (2022) demonstrated that subsea infrastructure — previously considered difficult to attack — is operationally reachable.

### ICS/SCADA Vulnerabilities
Industrial control systems managing energy infrastructure frequently run legacy software with limited patch cycles and poor network segmentation. Sandworm (GRU) demonstrated the ability to manipulate ICS systems to cause physical effects, including the 2016 Ukrainian power grid blackout affecting 230,000 customers.

### Ransomware and Extortion
Energy companies are frequent ransomware targets due to their tolerance for paying ransoms to restore operational continuity. Colonial Pipeline (2021) and multiple European energy operators have paid significant ransoms. Criminal groups with Russian nexus operate with implicit state tolerance.

### Supply Chain Interdependencies
European gas and oil markets are deeply interconnected. Disruption at a single major transit point — Ukrainian pipeline system, Norwegian processing facilities, Strait of Hormuz shipping lanes — can trigger cascading price and supply effects across multiple countries simultaneously.

## Known Incidents
- Stuxnet worm targeting Iranian uranium enrichment centrifuges (2010) — first confirmed ICS weapon
- Ukrainian power grid attacks by Sandworm (2015, 2016) — first confirmed cyberattack causing power outages
- Nord Stream 1 and 2 pipeline sabotage, Baltic Sea (September 2022)
- Colonial Pipeline ransomware (DarkSide, May 2021) — six-day shutdown of US East Coast fuel supply

## Intelligence Implications
Nation-state actors — primarily Russia and China — pre-position in energy sector networks for contingency use rather than immediate disruption. Collection priority is mapping dependencies and identifying single points of failure. Criminal ransomware represents a parallel threat with financial rather than strategic motivation.
