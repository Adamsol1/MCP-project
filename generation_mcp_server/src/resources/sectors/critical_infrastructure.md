# Critical Infrastructure Threats

## Strategic Overview
Critical infrastructure — power grids, water systems, transportation networks, financial systems, and healthcare — is a primary target for state-sponsored cyber operations and physical sabotage. The converging trends of operational technology (OT) connectivity, aging legacy systems, and adversary pre-positioning create an environment where major attacks on civilian infrastructure are a persistent risk. Russia, China, Iran, and North Korea all maintain active pre-positioned access in Western critical infrastructure for use in deterrence or conflict.

## Power Grid

### Russian Operations Against European Grids
Sandworm (GRU Unit 74455) has conducted the only confirmed cyber attacks causing physical power outages: Ukraine's Prykarpattiaoblenergo (December 2015, 225,000 customers affected) and Ukrenergo (December 2016). The Industroyer/Crashoverride malware used in the 2016 attack is designed to directly manipulate power grid industrial control systems and was adapted (Industroyer2) for use in April 2022 against Ukrainian grid operators.

### Volt Typhoon in US Infrastructure
US CISA, NSA, and Five Eyes partners confirmed in 2024 that Chinese state actor Volt Typhoon had pre-positioned access in US critical infrastructure — water utilities, power companies, communications providers, and transportation systems — specifically assessing the access as preparation for disruptive attacks in a Taiwan conflict scenario. This represents pre-conflict positioning, not active disruption.

## Water Systems
Water treatment and distribution systems are increasingly networked and are particularly vulnerable targets due to legacy OT systems rarely designed with cybersecurity in mind. Iran-linked actors attacked the Aliquippa, Pennsylvania water treatment facility (2023), gaining access to an Israeli-made Unitronics programmable logic controller. Hamas-linked operators accessed Israeli water authority networks. Water system attacks risk chemical dosing manipulation and can affect public health at scale.

## Transportation and Logistics
Transportation infrastructure attacks can rapidly disable economic activity. Key vectors include:
- **Rail**: Sabotage of track signalling systems; arson attacks on rail infrastructure in Germany and France (2024, attributed to Russian-linked actors)
- **Ports**: Cyber attacks on port management systems (NotPetya devastated Maersk's global operations in 2017)
- **Aviation**: Air traffic control system vulnerabilities; GPS spoofing affecting navigation

## Financial Sector
State-sponsored actors (primarily Russia's Sandworm and Iran's threat groups) have conducted SWIFT-targeting operations (Bangladesh Bank heist via North Korea), attempted manipulation of financial data rather than theft, and DDoS attacks against financial institutions. The financial system's interconnectedness means that a successful attack on a major clearing house or central bank could have systemic consequences.

## Intelligence Implications
Volt Typhoon's documented pre-positioning in US infrastructure for potential Taiwan conflict use represents a strategic deterrence asset from China's perspective — the threat of civilian disruption complicates US decision-making. Russia's demonstrated willingness to conduct destructive attacks on civilian infrastructure (Ukraine gas, water, power, 2022-2024) establishes precedent that NATO planners must account for. Physical sabotage by Russian intelligence against European infrastructure (arson, railway sabotage) has increased markedly since 2022.
