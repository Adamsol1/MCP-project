# China–Taiwan: Taiwan Strait Scenario

## Strategic Overview
Taiwan is the single most dangerous potential flashpoint in the Indo-Pacific and arguably globally. The People's Republic of China (PRC) claims Taiwan as an inalienable part of its territory and has never renounced the use of force to achieve "reunification." Taiwan's government (Republic of China) maintains a separate democratic state. The United States maintains a policy of "strategic ambiguity" — selling Taiwan defensive arms while not explicitly committing to military defence — which Xi Jinping's regime is increasingly testing.

## PLA Military Build-Up
The People's Liberation Army (PLA) has systematically developed capabilities for a Taiwan contingency since the 1995-1996 Taiwan Strait Crisis (when US carrier groups deterred PRC missile tests). Key capabilities include:
- **Amphibious lift**: Significant expansion of PLAN landing ships and civilian RO-RO vessels that can be requisitioned for military transport
- **A2/AD network**: DF-21D/DF-26 anti-ship ballistic missiles capable of threatening US carrier groups within the First Island Chain
- **Rocket Force**: Thousands of short-range ballistic missiles targeting Taiwan's military installations, airfields, and command centres
- **Cyber and EW**: Extensive pre-positioning of access in Taiwanese critical infrastructure for Day 1 operations

## Economic Interdependence as Complication
Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) produces approximately 90% of the world's most advanced semiconductors (below 5nm). Any conflict disrupting Taiwan's production would cause an immediate global technology crisis affecting everything from consumer electronics to weapons systems. This creates a paradox: Taiwan's strategic value makes it a target, but the economic consequences of conflict create deterrence.

## US Commitments and Strategic Ambiguity
The Taiwan Relations Act (1979) obligates the US to provide Taiwan with defensive arms. Multiple US presidents (Biden in particular) have verbally committed to defending Taiwan, though the White House has consistently walked back these statements. The US has increased arms sales to Taiwan (submarines, HIMARS, air defence) and authorised National Guard training relationships. The deployment of US troops to Taiwan for training missions signals a hardening posture.

## Cross-Strait Flashpoints
- **Median Line violations**: PLA aircraft routinely cross the Taiwan Strait median line, previously observed as a de facto boundary
- **Naval encirclements**: Large-scale PLA Navy and Air Force exercises surrounding Taiwan following US political visits (e.g., Pelosi 2022)
- **Information operations**: China conducts persistent influence operations targeting Taiwanese elections, media, and civil society

## Intelligence Implications
Western intelligence assesses a PLA forced reunification attempt as a rising risk through 2027-2030, when Xi Jinping is assessed to want the capability in place. Any Taiwan contingency would trigger immediate global supply chain disruption, US-China military confrontation, and cascading economic consequences. Regional states (Japan, Australia, South Korea) face secondary risk from being drawn in or sanctioned for neutrality.
