# EU–Russia Relations

## Strategic Overview
Russia's 2022 full-scale invasion of Ukraine fundamentally ruptured EU–Russia relations. The EU has since imposed twelve successive sanctions packages, largely severed energy ties, and positioned itself as a primary backer of Ukraine's defence. Russia considers the EU a hostile actor and has intensified hybrid operations against member states.

## Key Tension Points

### Energy Dependency (Declining)
Before 2022, Russia supplied approximately 40% of EU natural gas via pipelines including Nord Stream 1 and TurkStream. The EU's emergency gas diversification drive reduced Russian pipeline imports to under 10% by 2024. Russia weaponised supply cuts (Gazprom to Germany, Poland, Bulgaria) as economic coercion.

### Nord Stream Sabotage
The September 2022 destruction of Nord Stream 1 and 2 in the Baltic Sea demonstrated the vulnerability of undersea critical infrastructure. Attribution remains contested but multiple EU member states have conducted parallel investigations.

### Sanctions Evasion
Russia operates extensive parallel import networks through third countries (Turkey, UAE, Central Asia) to circumvent EU export controls on dual-use goods, semiconductors, and military components. This creates intelligence collection requirements across multiple jurisdictions.

### Hybrid Operations
GRU and SVR units conduct influence operations, sabotage, and cyberattacks across EU member states. Confirmed or attributed incidents include arson attacks on logistics nodes, recruitment of European nationals for deniable sabotage, and disinformation campaigns targeting elections.

## Known Incidents
- Arson and sabotage at logistics facilities in Germany, Poland, and the Czech Republic (2023–2024)
- GRU Unit 29155 linked to assassination attempts and destabilisation operations in multiple EU states
- Cyberattacks by APT28 and Sandworm against European energy grids, parliaments, and media

## Intelligence Implications
Russia's primary objectives are to fracture EU cohesion on Ukraine support, degrade defence-industrial capacity, and restore energy leverage. Hybrid tactics — combining cyber, sabotage, and information operations — are calibrated to remain below the threshold of Article 5 activation.
