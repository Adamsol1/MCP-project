# Iran–Israel Shadow War

## Strategic Overview
Iran and Israel are engaged in an undeclared multi-domain conflict spanning assassinations, sabotage, proxy warfare, cyber operations, and, since April 2024, direct military exchanges. Iran's stated objective is the elimination of the Israeli state; Israel's objective is prevention of Iranian nuclear capability and degradation of its proxy network. The conflict is fought primarily through proxies and deniable operations but has crossed into overt confrontation.

## Assassination and Sabotage Operations (Attributed)

### Israeli Operations Against Iran
- **Natanz nuclear facility explosions** (2020, 2021): Attributed to Israeli sabotage, causing significant centrifuge damage
- **Assassination of Mohsen Fakhrizadeh** (2020): Iran's top nuclear scientist killed by remote-operated weapon near Tehran
- **Assassination of IRGC generals** in Syria and Lebanon by Israeli airstrikes, including Quds Force commander Mohammad Reza Zahedi (2024)
- **Stuxnet worm** (2009–2010): US-Israeli joint operation that physically destroyed Iranian centrifuges via cyber means

### Iranian Operations Against Israel
- **Targeted killings** of Israeli intelligence officers and Mossad-linked individuals in third countries
- **Bomb attacks** on Israeli diplomatic missions and Jewish community centres globally, including Buenos Aires (1992, 1994)
- **Cyber operations** against Israeli water infrastructure (2020) and financial sector

## Proxy Warfare
Iran finances, arms, and trains the primary threats Israel faces on its borders:
- **Hezbollah**: Armed with precision-guided missiles capable of striking anywhere in Israel; engaged in ongoing cross-border fire since October 2023
- **Hamas and PIJ**: Iranian weapons pipelines via sea and through Sudan-Egypt smuggling routes
- **Houthis**: Drone and missile attacks on Israel from Yemen

## Direct Military Exchange — April/October 2024
For the first time in history, Iran conducted a direct ballistic missile and drone attack against Israeli territory (April 13, 2024) using approximately 170 drones, 120 ballistic missiles, and 30 cruise missiles — virtually all intercepted. Israel responded with a limited strike on Iranian air defence systems near Isfahan. A second Iranian ballistic missile barrage followed in October 2024. These exchanges marked the end of purely proxy-mediated conflict.

## Intelligence Implications
The Iran-Israel conflict imposes secondary intelligence risks on all parties with regional presence. Third-country citizens and infrastructure can be targeted in Israeli or Iranian retaliation cycles. The escalation to direct exchanges suggests a lowered threshold for future direct confrontation, particularly if Iran's nuclear programme approaches weapons assembly.
