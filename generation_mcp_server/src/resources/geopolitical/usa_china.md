# United States–China Relations

## Strategic Overview
The US formally characterises China as its primary strategic competitor. Competition spans military, technological, economic, and intelligence dimensions. Both sides maintain diplomatic channels to manage escalation, but the structural rivalry is assessed as long-term and deepening.

## Key Tension Points

### Taiwan Strait
China asserts Taiwan is a breakaway province to be reunified, by force if necessary. The US maintains strategic ambiguity — selling arms to Taiwan under the Taiwan Relations Act without committing to direct military intervention. Taiwan Strait crises (1996, 2022 Pelosi visit) have demonstrated high escalation potential. A Taiwan conflict would be the most significant geopolitical event since World War II.

### Semiconductor Export Controls
The US has implemented successive rounds of export controls targeting advanced semiconductors, chip-making equipment, and AI hardware to China (October 2022, October 2023). The goal is to slow China's military AI and advanced computing development. China is investing heavily in domestic alternatives but remains years behind the frontier.

### Volt Typhoon — Critical Infrastructure Pre-Positioning
CISA and NSA have jointly warned that Chinese state actor Volt Typhoon has pre-positioned in US critical infrastructure networks — utilities, water, communications, transportation — not for immediate disruption but to enable attacks in a future Taiwan conflict. This represents a strategic deterrence capability.

### Trade and Technology Competition
The US–China trade war (tariffs from 2018, expanded through 2024) and China's export controls on gallium and germanium (critical for semiconductors) illustrate the weaponisation of supply chains. Both sides are pursuing selective decoupling in strategically sensitive sectors.

## Known Incidents
- Volt Typhoon identified in US critical infrastructure including Guam communications (2023)
- Salt Typhoon compromise of US telecommunications providers, intercepting law enforcement wiretap systems (2024)
- OPM breach exposing 21.5 million US government personnel records, attributed to MSS (2015)

## Intelligence Implications
China's US-focused collection priorities are: military technology and readiness, policy deliberations on Taiwan, identification of intelligence assets, and economic/commercial intelligence. The Volt Typhoon pre-positioning indicates China is preparing operational infrastructure for a potential high-intensity conflict scenario.
