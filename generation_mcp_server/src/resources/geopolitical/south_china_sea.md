# South China Sea Territorial Disputes

## Strategic Overview
The South China Sea is one of the world's most contested bodies of water, carrying an estimated $3-5 trillion in annual trade. China claims approximately 90% of the sea under its "nine-dash line," rejecting a 2016 Permanent Court of Arbitration ruling that found the claim incompatible with UNCLOS. The disputed area overlaps with the Exclusive Economic Zones of Vietnam, the Philippines, Malaysia, Brunei, and Taiwan. The US conducts Freedom of Navigation Operations (FONOPs) to contest Chinese claims and operates regular naval patrols.

## Island-Building and Militarisation
China has transformed seven reefs and shoals in the Spratly Islands into artificial islands totalling approximately 3,200 acres of reclaimed land since 2013. Three have been developed into fully functional military bases (Fiery Cross Reef, Subi Reef, Mischief Reef) with:
- 3,000m runways capable of handling all military aircraft
- Hardened hangars for fighters, bombers, and maritime patrol aircraft
- Surface-to-air missile and anti-ship missile batteries
- Radar and intelligence collection facilities
- Port facilities capable of handling naval vessels

## Philippines Flashpoints
The Philippines, under the Marcos administration (2022-present), has revitalised the US-Philippines alliance and expanded US basing access (four additional EDCA sites added, including sites facing Taiwan and the South China Sea). Chinese Coast Guard and maritime militia vessels have physically blocked and water-cannoned Philippine resupply missions to BRP Sierra Madre at Second Thomas Shoal. The US has reaffirmed that the Mutual Defence Treaty covers Philippine armed forces, ships, and aircraft in the South China Sea.

## Vietnam and Malaysia
Vietnam disputes Chinese claims most aggressively after the Philippines, conducting periodic standoffs at oil blocks within its continental shelf. China's HD-981 drilling platform deployment into Vietnam's EEZ (2014) triggered anti-Chinese riots. Malaysia has avoided public confrontation while conducting low-key drilling operations in disputed blocks.

## Maritime Militia
China employs a "maritime militia" — fishing vessels crewed by paramilitary personnel operating under PLAN command — to assert presence and pressure rival claimants below the threshold of formal military action. These vessels occupy reefs, shadow other nations' survey ships, and provide intelligence collection while maintaining civilian cover.

## Intelligence Implications
Chinese military bases in the Spratlys provide persistent maritime domain awareness over all commercial and military shipping in the South China Sea. The bases extend Chinese A2/AD coverage and provide forward positioning for air and naval forces. Any escalation with the Philippines risks triggering US treaty obligations and a direct US-China naval confrontation in a highly congested maritime environment.
