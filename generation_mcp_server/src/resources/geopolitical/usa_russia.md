# United States–Russia Relations

## Strategic Overview
US–Russia relations are at their lowest point since the Cold War following Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine in February 2022. The US is Ukraine's largest single donor of military and financial aid. Russia views US involvement as a proxy war and has signalled nuclear escalation thresholds in response to specific weapon system transfers.

## Key Tension Points

### Sanctions Regime
The US has imposed the most extensive sanctions regime in history against Russia: blocking the Russian central bank's foreign reserves, excluding major Russian banks from SWIFT, and targeting energy, defence, and dual-use exports. Russia counters through third-country evasion networks and domestic substitution.

### NATO Expansion
Russia's stated casus belli for the Ukraine invasion included NATO's eastward expansion. Finland joined NATO in 2023 and Sweden in 2024, directly extending the Alliance's border with Russia by over 1,300 km. Russia has responded with military posturing in the Baltic and Arctic regions.

### Nuclear Posture
Russia has suspended participation in the New START treaty (2023) and conducted nuclear signalling at multiple escalation points in the Ukraine conflict. US doctrine treats a Russian nuclear strike — even tactical — as requiring a severe conventional response. Escalation management is a primary intelligence requirement.

### Arctic Competition
The US and Russia are the dominant Arctic powers by landmass. Both operate nuclear-powered submarines under Arctic ice. Russia's Northern Sea Route claims conflict with US freedom of navigation positions. Militarisation of the Arctic — bases, missile defence, hypersonic systems — is accelerating.

## Known Incidents
- Russian interference in 2016 US presidential election (GRU + Internet Research Agency)
- SolarWinds supply chain compromise by SVR (APT29), affecting US government networks (2020)
- Colonial Pipeline ransomware (DarkSide, Russia-based) causing fuel disruption across US East Coast (2021)

## Intelligence Implications
Russia's primary US-focused objectives are: undermining political cohesion and support for Ukraine, degrading NATO unity, intelligence collection against US military planning, and demonstrating cyber and hybrid capabilities as strategic deterrents. GRU, SVR, and FSB all maintain active operations targeting US persons and infrastructure.
