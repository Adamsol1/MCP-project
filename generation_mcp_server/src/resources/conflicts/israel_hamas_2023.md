# Israel–Hamas War (2023–present)

## Strategic Overview
Hamas's attack on Israel on 7 October 2023 — the deadliest single day for Jewish people since the Holocaust (approximately 1,200 killed, 251 taken hostage) — triggered Israel's most intense military campaign in Gaza. The war has reshaped Middle East security dynamics, strained Western alliances, triggered Hezbollah-Israel exchanges, drawn in Houthi and Iranian proxies, and produced a direct Iran-Israel military exchange in April and October 2024.

## Hamas Attack (7 October 2023)
Hamas launched a coordinated multi-axis assault on Israeli border communities, military bases, and a music festival. The operation involved approximately 3,000 militants breaching the border fence at multiple points, overrunning communities including Kibbutz Be'eri and Re'im, and taking 251 hostages to Gaza. The intelligence failure was significant — signals intelligence collection from Gaza had been deprioritised and Hamas operatives communicated through non-electronic methods.

## Israeli Military Campaign in Gaza
Israel declared war and launched a ground invasion of northern Gaza in October 2023, followed by southern Gaza operations. The campaign's stated objectives — dismantling Hamas's military infrastructure and recovering hostages — have been partially achieved at significant civilian cost. Key operations included:
- Destruction of Hamas's tunnel network (the "metro")
- Killing of senior Hamas military and political leadership (Yahya Sinwar killed October 2024)
- Significant civilian casualty toll creating international political pressure

## Hezbollah-Israel Northern Front
Hezbollah opened a "solidarity" front against Israel from Lebanon on 8 October 2023, engaging in daily cross-border exchanges. Escalation accelerated in 2024 with Israel targeting Hezbollah commanders (including Hassan Nasrallah, killed September 2024) and conducting a ground incursion into southern Lebanon. Hezbollah's precision missile arsenal was significantly degraded. A ceasefire was reached in November 2024.

## Regional Escalation Dynamics
- **Houthis**: Conducted over 100 attacks on commercial shipping in the Red Sea and drone/missile attacks on Israel
- **Iran-Israel direct exchange**: Iran launched direct ballistic missile attacks on Israel (April and October 2024) — unprecedented direct state-on-state military exchange
- **Syria and Iraq**: Iran-linked groups attacked US forces in Syria and Iraq; US conducted retaliatory strikes

## Intelligence Implications
The war demonstrated significant intelligence failures (Hamas attack surprise) and successes (elimination of Hamas and Hezbollah leadership). The conflict has drawn in Iran's full proxy network simultaneously — providing rare empirical data on how the "Axis of Resistance" coordinates. Red Sea shipping disruption has imposed significant global economic costs. The potential for Iran-Israel escalation to direct war remains elevated, with implications for global energy markets and regional stability.
