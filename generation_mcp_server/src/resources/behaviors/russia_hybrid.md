# Russia: Hybrid Warfare Doctrine and Behavioural Patterns

## Strategic Overview
Russia employs a doctrine of hybrid warfare — the coordinated use of political subversion, disinformation, cyber operations, economic coercion, proxy forces, and military posturing to achieve strategic objectives below the threshold of declared war. This doctrine, theorised by Russian military analysts as "new generation warfare" (Gerasimov Doctrine, though this attribution is disputed), has been systematically applied against Ukraine since 2014, against Western democracies through election interference, and against European infrastructure since 2022.

## Disinformation and Influence Operations
Russia operates the most sophisticated state disinformation apparatus in the world, comprising:
- **Internet Research Agency (IRA)**: St. Petersburg-based troll farm creating fake personas, amplifying divisive content, and running sock-puppet networks across Western social media platforms
- **RT (formerly Russia Today) and Sputnik**: State media operating in multiple languages targeting Western audiences with pro-Kremlin narratives, conspiracy theories, and anti-Ukraine content
- **Ghostwriter operation**: GRU-attributed campaign hacking websites of European media and politicians to post forged content under their names
- **Secondary Infektion**: Long-running network of fake personas and websites seeding forged documents into Western discourse

## Sabotage Operations in Europe (2022–present)
Since Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine, European security services have documented a significant increase in Russian-linked physical sabotage operations:
- **Arson attacks** on logistics and warehouse facilities in Lithuania, Poland, Germany, and the UK, linked to GRU-directed networks
- **Rail sabotage**: Signal box arson disrupting rail operations in Germany and France
- **Parcel bomb plots**: Incendiary packages targeting logistics companies (DHL, TNT) traced to GRU
- These operations are often carried out by proxy actors (paid intermediaries, often with criminal connections) to maintain deniability

## Election Interference
Russia conducts systematic operations to influence Western elections, amplifying extremist candidates, divisive narratives, and anti-NATO sentiment:
- US presidential elections (2016, 2020): IRA operations and GRU hacking/leaking (DNC, Macron campaign)
- French elections (2017): En Marche leaks operation
- German elections: Targeting of Bundestag members and mainstream parties
- Pattern: amplify far-right and far-left simultaneously to polarise; attack credibility of mainstream pro-NATO parties

## Energy Weaponisation
Russia systematically used energy supply as a coercive tool against Europe:
- Manipulation of gas deliveries through Ukraine (2006, 2009, 2014 crises)
- Reduction and eventual halt of Nord Stream pipeline gas supplies to Germany (2022)
- Maintenance of gas contracts with Hungary, Slovakia to create EU divisions
- Weaponisation strategy aimed at exhausting European political will to support Ukraine

## Intelligence Implications
Russian hybrid operations are designed to be deniable, cumulative, and ambiguous — no single act crosses the threshold for a military response. The pattern requires whole-of-government attribution and response frameworks. Russia's willingness to conduct physical sabotage in NATO members' territories (arson, cable cuts) since 2022 represents an escalation from purely informational and cyber operations and signals that Russia assesses low risk of escalation.
