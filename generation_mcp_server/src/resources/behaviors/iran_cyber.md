# Iran: Cyber Operations and Behaviour Patterns

## Strategic Overview
Iran has developed significant offensive cyber capabilities since Stuxnet (2009-2010) demonstrated to Iranian leadership that cyber weapons could cause physical damage to their critical infrastructure. Iran's cyber programme serves three primary objectives: retaliating against US and Israeli actions, projecting power against regional adversaries (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Israel), and targeting the Iranian diaspora and opposition figures globally. Iranian cyber operations combine destructive malware, espionage, and influence operations.

## Primary Threat Groups

### APT33 / Refined Kitten (IRGC-linked)
Focused on aerospace, energy (particularly petrochemical), and defence sectors. Known for spearphishing operations against Saudi, US, South Korean, and European targets. Associated with the Shamoon disk-wiping malware used against Saudi Aramco (2012, destroyed 35,000 workstations) and subsequent Shamoon iterations against Gulf energy companies (2016-2018).

### APT34 / OilRig / Helix Kitten (MOIS-linked)
Ministry of Intelligence and Security cyber unit. Long-running espionage operations against Middle East governments, financial institutions, and telecommunications companies. Known for DNS hijacking campaigns, credential harvesting, and use of DNS as command-and-control channel.

### Charming Kitten / APT42 (IRGC Intelligence)
Focused on targeting political dissidents, journalists, academics, and US/European policymakers. Conducts extensive social engineering using fake personas of journalists and academics to deliver phishing links. Responsible for multiple intrusion attempts against US presidential campaign staff (2024) and targeting of US officials in the Iran nuclear talks.

## Industrial Control System Attacks
Iran has demonstrated willingness to target OT/ICS environments beyond Stuxnet retaliation:
- **Bowman Avenue Dam** (New York, 2013): Iranian actors accessed a dam's SCADA systems
- **Aliquippa Water Authority** (Pennsylvania, 2023): Iranian-linked group Cyber Av3ngers compromised Israeli-made Unitronics PLCs in a US water utility
- **Saudi Aramco (Shamoon)**: While not pure OT, the scale of destruction demonstrated willingness to conduct disruptive attacks against energy sector

## Financial Sector Targeting
Operation Ababil (2012-2013): Iran conducted sustained DDoS attacks against US banks (Bank of America, JPMorgan, Citigroup, Wells Fargo) in retaliation for sanctions, generating 140 Gbps attack traffic — unprecedented at the time. Iran has continued developing DDoS capabilities and has conducted similar operations against Israeli financial institutions.

## Espionage Against Diaspora
Iran's MOIS and IRGC conduct extensive surveillance, intimidation, and harassment of Iranian dissidents, journalists, and opposition figures globally. This includes:
- Social media monitoring and fake persona infiltration of diaspora communities
- Kidnapping plots against dissidents in third countries (confirmed cases in US, Europe)
- Malware targeting phones of journalists covering Iran

## Intelligence Implications
Iran's cyber operations are closely calibrated to sanctions and geopolitical pressure cycles — escalating in response to specific US or Israeli actions. Iran has shown willingness to target civilian infrastructure, setting a lower escalation threshold than Russia or China. The IRGC's integration of proxy forces and cyber capabilities creates scenarios where cyber and kinetic operations are coordinated in the same campaign.
